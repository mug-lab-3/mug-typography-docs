PARAMETER REFERENCE

Camera Settings

Controls the viewpoint and projection method used to view 3D typography. Find the target, camera angle, perspective, zoom, and lens shift settings by purpose.

◎TargetCamera target point↘↻OrbitOrbit with Yaw and Pitch↘△PerspectivePerspective intensity↘⌕ZoomAdjust the projected size↘↔Lens ShiftTranslate the projection center↘✥Viewer ControlControl directly in the viewer↘

This camera settings page for 3D projection is displayed only for Mug Typography 3D and Mug Typography Lite (3D). Adjust the orientation of the text itself in Global / Character Settings, and adjust the viewpoint and projection method on this page.

Parameters ​

ParameterDescriptionDefault
TargetThe position the camera looks at. It uses the same normalized coordinates as Global Position, with 0.5, 0.5 at the center of the canvas.X0.5Y0.5
Camera YawThe horizontal angle around the target. Positive values move the camera to the right.0°
Camera PitchThe vertical angle around the target. Positive values look down from above.0°
Perspective StrengthThe intensity of the perspective effect. Higher values produce stronger trapezoidal distortion like a close, wide-angle view, while lower values produce a flatter, telephoto-like view.0.5
Camera ZoomChanges the size of the projected text while preserving perspective.1.0
Lens ShiftShifts the projection center along the X/Y axes without rotating the viewpoint. Positive Y moves it upward.X0.0Y0.0

Controlling the Camera in the Viewer ​

Set Selection Mode to Camera to display dedicated controls in the viewer.

ActionSetting Changed
Drag the Orbit handleCamera Yaw / Camera Pitch
WinAlt/MacOption + drag an empty areaCamera Yaw / Camera Pitch
Drag the Target PadTarget
Drag the Zoom handle left or rightCamera Zoom
Drag the Perspective handle left or rightPerspective Strength
Drag an empty area outside the controlsLens Shift
Hold Shift during any of the above drag operationsConstrain movement to the axis with the greater displacement

TIP

Perspective Strength is designed to change only the perspective while preserving the apparent size. Use Camera Zoom to adjust the size within the frame, and use Global Scale when you want to change the size of the typography itself.

Choosing Between Camera and Object Controls ​

  • Tilt all text in place: Global Yaw / Global Pitch
  • Tilt only a specific character: Character Yaw / Pitch
  • Move characters or parts forward or backward: Z Offset
  • Change the direction from which the scene is viewed: Camera Yaw / Pitch
  • Translate only the composition: Lens Shift
  • Change perspective: Perspective Strength
  • Change the size within the frame: Camera Zoom
